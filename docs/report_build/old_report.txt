1.1 Overview
In recent years, many students and expatriates in Egypt have faced challenges finding suitable housing due to the difficulty of locating reliable brokers or available apartments in new cities. Traditional methods often require physical visits, time-consuming searches, and dealing with intermediaries who may not always be trustworthy. These obstacles highlight the need for a digital platform that simplifies both the renting and buying processes, providing users with a more accessible and transparent housing experience.
The proposed Broker Website serves as a comprehensive online solution that connects property owners, brokers, and seekers--whether they are looking to rent or purchase apartments--in an efficient and trustworthy way. Through a simple registration process, users can create accounts and choose their roles as brokers who list their properties, or seekers who wish to rent or buy. Once logged in, users can browse apartment listings that include detailed information such as price, location, property features, and ownership type (rent or sale), with advanced filtering options to refine search results.
The system supports both private and shared housing, allowing users to find standard apartments for rent or sale, or shared units that cater especially to students and individuals relocating from other cities. In the shared housing section, the platform displays short profiles of current residents to help users identify compatible roommates and make informed decisions before contacting the property owner or broker.
Additionally, the platform integrates an AI-powered assistant to enhance the overall experience. By analyzing user inputs such as preferred location, price range, apartment type, ownership preference (rent or buy), and the number of roommates, the AI refines search results and suggests the most suitable options. This intelligent feature helps users save time and effort, ensuring a faster, smarter, and more personalized experience when renting or purchasing properties online.
1.2 Motivation
Finding suitable housing has become an increasingly complex challenge for individuals in Egypt, especially students, workers relocating to new cities, and families searching for secure and affordable options. Traditional rental processes still depend on brokers, personal networks, or random online posts--methods that are often time-consuming, unreliable, and lacking transparency. These outdated practices make property searching stressful and inefficient, particularly as demand continues to rise across many cities.
The introduction of Egypt's New Rental Law of 2025 has further emphasized the need for modern digital housing solutions. With the new law restructuring old rental contracts, redefining rent values, and setting clear timelines for contract termination, many tenants and property owners have entered a period of transition. This shift has pushed a

significant number of people to search for new accommodation or alternative housing arrangements. As a result, the demand for platforms that provide clarity, updated rental opportunities, and easier access to available units has noticeably increased--especially for those affected by the changes in long-term rental regulations.
To address these challenges, the Broker Website was developed as a centralized, transparent, and user-friendly platform that simplifies the entire real estate experience. The system allows users to explore verified listings for rent or sale, communicate directly with owners or trusted brokers, and access detailed property information without relying on inconsistent traditional methods.
What makes the platform especially relevant is its strong support for shared housing and shared units, which have become essential for students and individuals searching for affordable living options. The system enables users to browse shared rooms, understand occupant preferences, and match with suitable living arrangements--something missing from most current platforms in Egypt.
Additionally, the integration of an AI-assisted recommendation system significantly enhances the search journey. Users can enter their preferences, and the system automatically suggests suitable properties--whether full apartments or shared units-- based on personalized criteria. This intelligent feature reduces search time, improves accuracy, and helps users make more confident housing decisions in a market undergoing legal and economic change.

1.4 Aim
The main aim of this project is to develop a comprehensive online real estate broker platform that simplifies and modernizes the process of renting and purchasing apartments in Egypt. The platform serves as a digital bridge connecting property owners, brokers, and seekers, reducing dependency on traditional intermediaries and minimizing the effort required for physical property searches. Through an intuitive and accessible interface, users can easily register, log in, and either list their properties for rent or sale, or browse available options according to their preferences.
In addition, the system integrates an AI-powered assistant designed to enhance the user experience by generating accurate, efficient, and personalized property recommendations based on user input and preferences. The overall goal is to deliver a secure, intelligent, and user-friendly platform that promotes transparency, trust, and convenience within the Egyptian real estate market.
1.5 Scope
Functional Scope:
The proposed system focuses on digitizing and modernizing the real estate process in Egypt by providing a centralized online platform that connects renters, buyers, brokers, and property owners. It enables users to register, authenticate, and select their roles--allowing brokers and owners to list their properties for rent or sale, while seekers can browse, filter, and apply for suitable options. The platform supports both private and shared housing, displaying essential property details such as price, location, type, and owner information. Additionally, an AI-assisted recommendation feature refines search results and suggests the most suitable rental or purchase opportunities based on user preferences.
Technical Scope: The project is designed as a web-based application built with modern technologies to ensure scalability, performance, and data security. It utilizes a relational database to store user and property information, supported by secure login, encryption, and verification protocols to protect sensitive data. The system integrates AI models for intelligent

User Scope:
The platform primarily targets four key user groups: brokers, property owners, renters, and buyers, with optional integration for institutional partners such as universities. Brokers and owners can create verified accounts, list their properties for rent or sale, and manage listings efficiently. Renters and buyers can explore available properties, use advanced filters to narrow down options, and contact property owners or brokers directly through the platform. This multi-user structure ensures inclusivity, flexibility, and scalability to accommodate various housing needs across Egypt's real estate market.

Despite its potential benefits, the proposed system faces several constraints that must be carefully considered during its design and implementation phases. These limitations may influence the platform's performance, accuracy, and scalability. The main constraints are outlined below:
À Dependence on User-Generated Content: The accuracy and reliability of property listings largely depend on the information submitted by brokers, owners, and users. Inaccurate or misleading data could affect trust and system credibility. Therefore, implementing strong verification and approval mechanisms is essential to maintain content quality.
À AI Recommendation Limitations: The effectiveness of the AI-assisted recommendation feature relies on the quality and completeness of available data. Limited datasets or inconsistent user inputs may reduce the precision and relevance of generated property suggestions.
À Infrastructure and Network Constraints: The system's performance and scalability depend on the quality of hosting infrastructure and internet connectivity. In regions with unstable or limited network coverage, user experience may be affected by slower response times or limited accessibility.
À Data Security and Privacy: As the platform handles sensitive personal and property-related information, ensuring robust data protection is critical. Unauthorized access, data breaches, or misuse of information must be prevented through secure authentication, encryption, and database management practices.

3.2 Analysis and limitations of existing system
Analysis of Existing System:
Existing real estate platforms--such as DUBBIZLE Egypt, Bayut Egypt, Sakneen, Weetas, Student.com, HousingAnywhere, Uniplaces, and AmberStudent--have contributed positively to improving accessibility within the housing market by offering various useful features.
Wide Property Listings: Platforms like Dubbizle and Bayut provide large databases of rental and sale listings across different cities and price ranges.
Direct Communication: Most websites allow direct contact between renters, owners, or brokers, helping users communicate quickly without traditional intermediaries.
Basic Search Filters: Existing systems offer fundamental filtering options such as location, price range, property type, and furnishing status.
Verified Listings (in some platforms): Platforms like Sakneen, Student.com, and AmberStudent offer partially verified or agency-managed listings for more reliability.
Dedicated Student Housing (international platforms): Websites such as Student.com, Uniplaces, and AmberStudent specialize in student accommodation, providing options close to universities with secure booking processes.
Broker/Agent Support: Platforms like Weetas and Bayut present professionally curated listings managed by certified agents.
Online Property Browsing: Most systems allow users to view photos, descriptions, and general details without needing physical visits.
Limitations of Existing System:
Despite the features offered, current platforms still suffer from major drawbacks that prevent them from fully addressing the needs of users--especially students and locals in Egypt.
Lack of AI Personalization: No platform provides AI-powered recommendations that match users with properties based on budget, location, preferences, or roommate compatibility.

Weak Verification & Fraud Risk: Platforms like DUBBIZLE lack strong identity verification, resulting in fake listings, misleading information, and scams.
Poor Support for Shared Housing & Gender-Specific Options: Most platforms do not support roommate matching, shared-unit browsing, or gender-filtered listings--highly demanded by students and newcomers.
Limited Transparency: Users often receive incomplete information regarding ownership, property condition, or contract expectations, leading to uncertainty and misinformation.
No Smart Filtering or Behavioral Matching: Filters on existing platforms are basic and do not adapt to user behavior or learning preferences.
Inadequate Safety and Secure Communication: Messaging is often external (WhatsApp, phone calls), meaning there is no secure, monitored communication environment.
3.3 Need for new system
Finding suitable housing in Egypt has become increasingly challenging, particularly for students, expatriates, and individuals relocating to new cities. Traditional methods--such as visiting brokers in person, relying on word-of-mouth, or browsing scattered classifieds--are often time-consuming, inefficient, and unreliable. Many users struggle to find trustworthy listings, updated rental prices, or compatible roommates, especially with the rising demand for affordable housing.
The introduction of Egypt's New Rental Law of 2025 further increased the pressure on the housing market. As old rental contracts are restructured, rental values change, and timelines for contract termination become clearer, many tenants have been pushed to search for new accommodation. This shift created a noticeable spike in housing demand, highlighting the need for a digital system that provides variety, transparency, and easy access to updated rental opportunities.
A new system is required to address these challenges by:
Providing Comprehensive Listings
Current methods lack a unified platform that offers reliable details about pricing, location, rental mode, and property features. The proposed system will present complete and verified listings, including options for shared units, which are essential for students and individuals seeking affordable living arrangements.

Improving Reliability and Trust By connecting seekers directly with verified property owners and trusted brokers, the platform reduces misinformation and protects users from fake or outdated listings--issues that have become more common as rental regulations shift. Expanding Accessibility An online platform allows users to browse apartments and shared rooms from anywhere, including newly relocated residents affected by the rental law changes. This makes the searching process easier, faster, and more accessible across different cities. Saving Time Through Smart Assistance Advanced search filters and an AI-powered assistant will help users quickly identify the most suitable properties--whether full apartments or shared units--reducing time, effort, and uncertainty in a rapidly evolving housing environment.

4.1 Design and Implementation Constraints
4.1.1 Technical Constraints
À Server Performance and Load Handling: As a web-based platform, all user interactions, property searches, and AI-based recommendations are processed on the server side. The system must handle multiple concurrent users, especially during peak browsing times. Limited hosting resources may affect response time and system availability.
À AI Recommendation Limitations: The accuracy of the AI-powered property recommendation system depends on the quality and completeness of user input data (location, budget, apartment type, and preferences). Incomplete or inaccurate data can reduce the effectiveness of personalized recommendations.
À Internet Dependency: The Broker Website requires a stable internet connection for browsing listings, uploading property images, and receiving AI-based suggestions. Users in areas with poor connectivity may experience slower performance or limited access.
À Browser and Device Compatibility: The system must function correctly across major web browsers (Chrome, Firefox, Edge, Safari) and on different devices (desktop, laptop, tablet, and mobile). User interface components, search filters, and listing displays must be responsive and consistent.
À Database and Storage Constraints: The platform relies on a relational database to store user accounts, property listings, images, and shared housing profiles. Storage limits and database performance may restrict the number of listings and images that can be handled efficiently.
37

4.1.2 Regulatory and Legal Constraints
À Data Privacy and Security: The system stores sensitive user data such as personal information, contact details, and property ownership records. It must comply with Egyptian data protection and privacy regulations, ensuring data encryption, secure authentication, and restricted access.
À Real Estate Compliance: All property listings must follow Egyptian real estate laws, including correct representation of ownership, rental agreements, and pricing. False or misleading listings must be prevented through verification and moderation mechanisms.
À Shared Housing and Cultural Regulations: Shared housing listings must respect cultural and social norms, such as gender-based preferences and privacy rules. User profiles in shared apartments must display only necessary information.
4.1.3 Resource Constraints
À Development Resources: The system is developed by a small student team, which limits the speed of development and the complexity of advanced features such as real-time messaging, payment systems, and automated verification. À Budget Limitations: The project is developed within a limited budget, which requires using low-cost domain services, shared web hosting, and open-source development tools. The website, database, and property images are hosted on affordable servers, which limits storage capacity, performance, and the number of users that can be supported. External AI APIs are used instead of building complex in-house AI models to reduce overall development and operational costs. À Time Constraints: The project must be completed within the academic semester, which limits the amount of testing, large-scale deployment, and advanced feature development. Therefore, the system focuses on implementing the essential functions such as user accounts, property listings, search and filtering, shared housing support, and AI-based recommendations.

4.2 Assumptions and Dependencies
4.2.1 Assumptions
À User Access to Internet and Devices: It is assumed that users (brokers, property owners, and seekers) have access to a modern web browser on a desktop, laptop, or mobile device with a stable internet connection to use the platform.
À Accuracy of Property Information: The system assumes that brokers and property owners provide accurate and complete property details, including price, location, ownership type (rent or sale), and apartment features.
À Image and Listing Format Compliance: It is assumed that uploaded property images and descriptions follow the required formats (e.g., JPEG, PNG) to ensure correct display on the website.
À Availability of Hosting and AI Services: The system assumes continuous availability of web hosting services and external AI APIs used for search enhancement and property recommendations.
4.2.2 Dependencies
À Web Hosting and Database Services: The platform depends on third-party hosting providers to store the website, backend system, and MySQL database for users and property listings.
À External AI APIs: The system relies on external AI services to generate personalized apartment and housing recommendations based on user preferences.
À Internet Connectivity: Both users and servers must have stable internet connections to allow browsing, searching, and data exchange.
À Verification and Moderation Processes: The system depends on administrative and verification processes to ensure the accuracy and legality of property listings and user accounts.
À Legal and Regulatory Compliance: The operation of the platform depends on compliance with Egyptian real estate laws and data protection regulations.

4.3 Risks and Risk Management
4.3.1 Technical Risks
À Incorrect or Poor AI Recommendations: The AI-based recommendation system may generate inaccurate or irrelevant property suggestions due to incomplete user data or limited historical information.  Risk Treatment: Improve input validation, refine recommendation rules, and allow users to manually search and filter properties alongside AI suggestions.
À Server Downtime and Performance Issues: The system may experience slow response times or downtime because of limited hosting resources or high user traffic.  Risk Treatment: Use reliable hosting services, apply database optimization, and perform regular system monitoring and backups.
À Browser and Device Compatibility Issues: Users may face problems when accessing the platform through different browsers or mobile devices.  Risk Treatment: Apply responsive design and conduct cross-browser and device testing before deployment.
4.3.2 Security and Privacy Risks
À User Data Exposure: Sensitive information such as user profiles, contact details, and property data may be at risk of unauthorized access.  Risk Treatment: Use HTTPS, secure login mechanisms, encrypted storage, and access control policies.
À Fraudulent or Fake Listings: Some users may submit misleading or fake property advertisements.  Risk Treatment: Implement user verification and listing approval processes before publishing properties.
40

4.3.3 Operational Risks À Low User Trust and Adoption: Users may hesitate to use the platform due to trust or usability concerns.  Risk Treatment: Introduce verified accounts, clear property information, and transparent communication features. À Limited Technical and Financial Resources: Budget and time constraints may delay development or restrict system improvements.  Risk Treatment: Use open-source tools, low-cost hosting, and phased development to focus on core features first.
4.3.4 Ethical and Market Risks À Bias in Property Recommendations: The AI system may prefer certain areas or price ranges due to unbalanced data.  Risk Treatment: Review recommendation logic regularly and ensure diversity in listing data. À Over-Reliance on AI Suggestions: Users may depend too much on AI recommendations and ignore manual searching.  Risk Treatment: Clearly label AI results as suggestions and provide full manual search and filtering options.
